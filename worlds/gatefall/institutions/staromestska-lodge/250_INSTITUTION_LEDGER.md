## Staroměstská Lodge

The Staroměstská Lodge is the co-op that gives an unaffiliated Prague hunter somewhere to start — the likeliest first home for a newly licensed awakened with no guild and no name yet, in a city where individuals matter sooner than anywhere in America. It runs the bulk of the freelance postings along the Metro Corridor (ENT-000095), the unglamorous low-rank contract floor beneath Karlov Guild's contracts and the internationals' ambitions, in service tunnels and disused platform ends most tourists never see.

### Interior Factions

**The Metro Regulars** — hunters who work the Metro Corridor day in and day out, and want the Lodge to stay exactly what it is: a practical, no-frills source of contract postings and tunnel-safety intel for people who need to eat this month.

**The Old Town Hopefuls** — newer, more ambitious members chasing a way into the Old Town Gate Cluster's (ENT-000094) better-paid heritage work, or onto Karlov Guild's (ENT-000104) radar entirely, who want the Lodge to invest its limited resources in training and reputation-building instead.

The Metro Regulars think the Lodge exists to keep freelancers alive and paid, not to run a farm system for Karlov; the Old Town Hopefuls think refusing to develop anyone past the tunnels is how the Lodge stays small forever.

### What They Control

The bulk of freelance contract postings along the Metro Corridor (ENT-000095); an informal reputation network that matters enormously in a hunter scene of only about 350 licensed people, where a new face is noticed within a week.

### What They Want

To keep giving unaffiliated Prague hunters a floor to work from without losing every promising member the moment Karlov or one of the internationals notices them; to keep its people out of the stronger black market that runs beneath Prague's economy and feeds artifacts and cores westward, which preys hardest on freelancers with no institutional backing.

### Current Tensions

Horizon Guild — Europe's (ENT-000105) aggressive Prague hiring draws directly on the Lodge's membership, the same pool the Old Town Hopefuls want the Lodge to actively groom for exactly that kind of attention. Meanwhile the black market's westward pull is a constant undertow beneath the Metro Corridor's thin pay, and the Metro Regulars argue that chasing prestige work is a distraction from the more basic problem of keeping members solvent enough that the black market never looks like the better offer.
