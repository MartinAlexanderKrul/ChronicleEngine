## Active Objectives (CLOSED)

**OBJ-1 — Secure a footing at the Athenaeum.**
Present the letter of introduction (`ENT-000020`) at the Athenaeum (`ENT-000003`); obtain permission to study.
Status: **FAILED** — Ilse presented the letter to Corvane and accessed the Athenaeum directly. However, her commitment to the double-agent strategy and her assault at the Order's hospice on Day 13 broke any possibility of continuing Athenaeum affiliation. The objective was abandoned when she refused legal options and was executed.

**OBJ-2 — Investigate the marsh-fever finding.**
Examine Corvane's evidence (the treatise and maps, `ENT-000015`) and form an independent view of the finding (`ENT-000013`).
Status: **COMPLETED BUT UNACTED** — Ilse examined Corvane's evidence critically, conducted independent field investigation, and concluded: tainted water is the fever vector. Her personal medical conviction was certain. However, she never acted on this conclusion (never advised against the wells publicly, never endorsed the finding publicly, never contributed her expertise to the investigation). The investigation itself stalled when she was detained. At time of death, the question of the fever's true cause remained unresolved in Halden.

**OBJ-3 — Manage the Order relationship and institutional pressure.**
Work without violating the Order's ban on teaching the finding (`EVT-000006`) or its civic authority in Halden (`REL-000010`); or navigate the conflict if direct violation becomes unavoidable.
Status: **FAILED — INVERTED** — Ilse attempted to manage through double-agent positioning (accept Order recruitment as cover). This strategy lasted less than 24 hours. She violently attacked the Prelate, refused all legal options, and was executed as a consequence. The original objective of navigating institutional pressure was not merely failed but inverted into direct institutional elimination of the protagonist.

All three objectives remain substantively unachieved. Ilse's death represents a terminal failure state for this campaign instantiation.
