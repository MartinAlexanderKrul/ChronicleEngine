## Scene 1: Morning at Association HQ

**[Setup Scene]**

You're standing at the Hunters' Association HQ in downtown Verholt. You've just completed your licensing ceremony this morning. Your E-Rank certificate is in hand, still warm from the official seal. Captain Marissa Thorne handed you the assignment briefing for Warehouse 7 just moments ago.

The morning sun is bright. The Association courtyard is alive: dozens of voices overlapping, hunters checking reinforced gear, the clank of equipment being readied, the hum of mana-saturated cores resonating in transport carts. Two D-Rank hunters are arguing about route tactics for what sounds like a C-tier site. A scout in the corner has a nasty bruise on her jaw—came back hot from something.

The Industrial District is thirty minutes south by foot. The Rift was detected only two days ago. You have most of the standard 2-7 day window. Six days remaining before critical collapse, the briefing said.

## Scene 5: Decision Point — Solo or Supervised

**[Player Action]** You decide to go solo. You find Captain Thorne and commit.

**[Narrative]**

Captain Marissa Thorne is in the briefing corridor reviewing assignment papers with a scout. She looks up as you approach—E-Rank hunter, still damp with gym sweat, clearly just come from training.

"Daedalus," she says. Not a question. "Decision made?"

You meet her eyes. "Solo. I'm going in alone."

She studies you for a moment. Not judgmental. Just assessing. Then she nods.

"Alright. Standard briefing stands—warehouse layout, Broodlings and Swarmers, core is in the deep chamber. You clear the core, Rift collapses. If you get in trouble, there's an evacuation stone in your equipment pack—breaks the seal from inside, pulls you out. One use only. Don't waste it unless you're actually dying." She hands you a small crystal—smooth, warm, thrumming faintly with mana. "This is it."

You pocket it. One lifeline.

"You've got daylight," Thorne continues. "Industrial District is thirty minutes. Get there, scout the perimeter, make the call when you're ready. Rift's been stable for two days—no sudden pressure spikes expected. But Rifts are Rifts. Stay sharp."

She doesn't wish you luck. Doesn't say "be careful." She just nods, professional, and goes back to her papers.

**You've committed to solo.**

## Scene 6: Journey to Warehouse 7

**[Player Action]** You walk to the warehouse on foot.

**[Narrative]**

You leave the Association HQ and turn south toward the Industrial District.

The afternoon sun is hot on your shoulders. The streets of central Verholt are busy—merchants, workers, civilians going about their day. Your E-Rank emblem catches eyes. People unconsciously step aside.

The city changes gradually. Market quarter gives way to merchant warehouses, guild headquarters, banking houses. The crowd thins. The air gets drier, dustier. You pass a tavern called the Crossbow—hunters cluster outside, comparing notes on assignments. No one you know. No one pays particular attention.

Your high Perception picks up details: a scout posting a new alert on a board (C-grade Rift confirmed northwest, 8-day window, team assembly in progress). Scorch marks on old buildings—remnants of past Rift collapses, probably. The way the street layout changes subtly, like the city's bones remember where danger has been.

By kilometer three, you're fully in the Industrial District. Larger buildings, older, more functional. Warehouses with numbers painted on them. Forges and manufacturing shops. The smell of metal and coal. The river is close.

At kilometer three, you see the cordoning.

**Warehouse 7** sits on the eastern edge of a cluster of industrial buildings, about fifty meters back from a main street. The Rift's perimeter is marked with Association tape—yellow and black, cordoned off in a wide circle. Two Association scouts are stationed at the nearest checkpoint, both D-Rank based on their bearing. They're watching, alert but not tense.

Beyond the tape, the warehouse itself: three-story brick, industrial, functional. The entrance is sealed with reinforced plating. But there's a secondary entrance on the side—that's where the Rift actually *is*, the briefing said. A lower-level storage entrance where the dimensional rupture has manifested.

The air around the Rift entrance shimmers faintly if you look at it directly. Even from fifty meters away, you can *feel* it—a pressure in the mana around you, like the air is slightly thicker in that direction. Your Perception notices everything: the way nearby plants seem slightly wilted, the way birds don't perch on the warehouse roof, the faint sound of skittering movement from inside (Broodlings, probably).

One of the scouts spots you and nods—they recognize the E-Rank emblem. They don't approach. They just watch to make sure you're authorized.

You're standing at the perimeter tape, fully mana'd (13/13), trained, holding an evacuation stone and a sword and the weight of your own decision.

**The Rift entrance is right there. Thirty meters away. The secondary entrance door is still sealed.**

## Scene 7: Entry Into Warehouse 7

**[Player Action]** You take a long breath and enter.

**[Narrative]**

The two scouts watch you approach. One of them nods slightly—acknowledgment. They don't stop you. This is what you signed up for.

The sealed warehouse door is reinforced steel, heavy, marked with the Association's administrative stamp. There's a keystone embedded in the frame—you press your E-Rank certificate against it, and the seal *hisses*. The door swings inward.

The pressure hits you immediately.

It's not painful, but it's *present*—a thickness in the air, a pressure in your mana, like stepping into water but you're breathing it. The air tastes slightly metallic. Your eyes water as they adjust. The sunlight from behind you doesn't penetrate far.

You step through the threshold.

The door swings shut behind you with a heavy *clang*. Sunlight cuts off. For a moment, total darkness—then your eyes adjust and the Rift's own strange luminescence begins: a faint, sourceless glow coming from deep inside the warehouse, sickly blue-green, casting everything in shadow and phosphorescence.

The warehouse interior stretches ahead: rows of storage shelves line both sides, metal frameworks rusted and sagging. Crates, scattered equipment, old machinery—all covered in that sickly luminescence. The floor is concrete, cracked in places, and you notice immediately the cracks are *glowing*—the Rift's mana bleeding through.

The air hums. Not sound exactly—a vibration at the edge of perception, the mana itself resonating.

You move forward carefully, hand on your sword hilt. Your fire-sense is alive—you can feel the latent heat in your core, the mana ready to flow. Your Perception catches everything.

As you move deeper past the first row of shelves, you see them.

They screech—a sound that cuts through the warehouse air like a blade. High-pitched, hungry.

---

**[Player Action]** "I don't hesitate. I use fireball."

**[Resolution]**

You don't hesitate.

The moment you see them, you're moving—fingers already forming the gesture, drawing mana from your core. The two Broodlings' heads track you, their legs tensing for an attack, but you're already *pulling*.

**Fireball.**

Twelve mana flows out of you like opening a door. The two spinning spheres of compressed fire manifest in the space between your hands. The 2-second cast time feels like forever and an instant at once. The Broodlings screech again, higher this time—alarm or hunger.

You complete the cast and *thrust*.

**[Outcome]**

The fireball erupts from your hands like a hurled stone wrapped in flame, spinning, building intensity as it arcs toward the crate stack. The trajectory is *perfect*—your high Mana Affinity guides the spell true. Stationary targets. Easy mark.

The fireball *impacts* on the crate stack below the Broodlings, detonating in a burst of orange-red heat and flame.

The explosion is *immediate and devastating*.

The crate splinters. The Broodlings are *engulfed*—one screams a sound that cuts off abruptly as the flames consume it. The second launches upward in reflex, too late, and the heat catches it mid-air. Both are *burning*, carapaces blackening, legs convulsing. They fall from the crate stack in twisted, smoking forms and hit the concrete hard.

They twitch once. Twice.

Then they stop moving.

**Mana: 13 → 1/13.**
