# The Ledger — System Reference (Reikon, Draft)

**Ledger:** World-layer reference lore for Reikon's diegetic System. Narrative reference, not an Object-Block canonical ledger.
**Status:** DRAFT — non-canonical world reference.

> **Compliance note (Decision 051).** Everything below is the *world's diegetic representation* of Chronicle Engine's emergent simulation, not a second set of mechanics. Action resolution is always intent + circumstance + d100 (Rules Section 4); growth is always lived (Rules Section 5); every Grade, Aspect, Mark, and number is in-world **information** (Rules Section 2.2) — reflected capability, never granted power. The Ledger *displays*; it never *decides*.

---

# The Page

Every Awakened bears a private **page** only they can see. It shows four things:

- a **Grade** — their overall standing;
- **Aspects** — broad facets of what they can do;
- **Marks** — specific learned skills and techniques;
- **Progression** — a filling measure that culminates in an **Ascension** (the "level-up") when they have genuinely grown.

The Dormant majority have no page and see nothing. No one can read another's page directly; what the world *knows* of a hunter's power is the Authority's **Grade** — an assessment, and therefore fallible, contestable, and forgeable.

---

# Grades

The Hunters' Authority grades licensed hunters from assessed deeds, not from the private page. The ladder is original to Reikon:

| Grade | Rough meaning |
|-------|---------------|
| **Grade V** | Newly licensed; clears shallow Rifts under supervision |
| **Grade IV** | Competent solo clearer of minor Rifts |
| **Grade III** | Team-capable; handles serious Rifts (Sergeant Roan's tier) |
| **Grade II** | Elite; fronts dangerous clears and public reputation (Corin Vask's tier) |
| **Grade I** | National-threat capable; a handful exist |
| **Anomalous** | Off-scale: a page with no clear ceiling. Vanishingly rare, watched, and coveted |

A Grade is a **claim**. It can lag behind real growth, be inflated by a guild, forged by a fixer (the forging of Grades, `230_KNOWLEDGE_SUBJECTS.md`), or simply be wrong — and a Rift does not care what the paper says.

---

# Aspects

Aspects are the page's broad facets — diegetic names for demonstrated capability. A typical page shows: **Power**, **Reflex**, **Focus**, **Presence**, **Vitality**, **Will**. They read high or low as the bearer has actually proven them, and in play they function as ordinary **Modifiers** (Rules Section 4.4) — advantages that shape resolution, never overrides that replace it.

---

# Marks and Classes

- **Marks** are specific learned skills and techniques — a guard stance, a blade art, a way of sealing a wound, an invocation. A Mark is a **Knowledge Asset** (Rules Sections 7.2, 8): it must be learned and practiced, taught or discovered, and it can be licensed, restricted, or illicit. Marks are not bought with Progression.
- **Classes** are recognized progression paths that organize which Marks and Aspects a hunter develops. Opening a desirable Class typically needs tutelage, guild sponsorship, or a rite, and the powerful ones are gatekept. The **Warden Class** — binding Rift-monsters rather than killing them — is outlawed and condemned (`230_KNOWLEDGE_SUBJECTS.md`); its true reach is disputed, and it remains bounded by ordinary resolution like any other path.

---

# Progression and Ascension

The Progression measure fills as the bearer does hard, real things — and empties into nothing if they do not. An **Ascension** is the page's announcement that genuine growth has crossed a threshold: a felt, luminous moment, a new Grade or a sharpened Aspect.

Per Decision 051, **the growth comes first and the Ascension surfaces it** — never the reverse. Grinding trivial Rifts by rote fills no bar; the Ledger cannot be farmed without real ordeal, practice, and risk. The Ascension is a reward *signal*, not a reward *mechanism*.

---

# Rifts

A **Rift** is a wound in the world that breathes monsters. Its life runs in three stages:

1. **Open** — a stable tear; monsters trickle out; the interior can be entered and cleared.
2. **Deepen** — left too long, a Rift grows; its interior enlarges and its monsters worsen. (The Kessler Rift is deepening; `210_PLACES.md`.)
3. **Collapse** — a Rift that is never cleared eventually bursts, disgorging its whole interior into the streets at once. The Danesmoor collapse is what that costs (`210_PLACES.md`, `260_SEED_EVENTS.md`).

The Authority **grades** Rifts by estimated danger, matches hunters to them, and answers to the public when the estimate is wrong. Clearing a Rift means reaching and closing its core before it deepens past reach.

---

# Cores and the Economy

Slain monsters and closed Rifts yield **cores** — dense residues that power industry, medicine, and the making of Marked relics. Cores are Reikon's second currency and the Meridian Guild's foundation: the Guild fields hunters, banks the cores, and corners the trade. A **deep-Rift core** is rare and strange enough to be worth a scandal — which is exactly the knot the Institute, the Vigil, and the Guild are now caught in (`240_RESOURCES.md`).

---

# Threats

Rift-monsters are catalogued loosely, by what they cost to fight rather than by species:

- **Broodlings** — numerous, weak, dangerous only in mass;
- **Breakers** — the standard clearance threat; a Grade IV–III problem;
- **Wardens** *(no relation to the outlawed Class)* — rare, intelligent, territorial; the reason deep Rifts kill teams;
- **Apex** — a Rift's core-beast; killing or sealing it closes the Rift.

The taxonomy is intentionally thin — a scaffold to fill in play, never a bestiary that resolves combat on the monster's behalf.

# Running the Ledger in Play (note for the Runtime)

- Surface the page **diegetically** and on the player's terms: a hunter may glance at their own page, feel an Ascension, or read an official Grade. Keep it in-fiction; it is Reikon's native interface, not an out-of-character dashboard.
- Announce an Ascension **after** the character has demonstrably grown in canon, never to grant the growth (Decision 051; Progression Surfacing in the Gameplay Runtime Profile).
- Treat Grades and Aspects as **Modifiers and information**: they color odds and shape how NPCs treat a hunter, but the die and the circumstance decide (Rules Section 4). The impossible stays impossible whatever the page says (Rules Section 4.2).
- Let the page be **wrong**: forged, inflated, misread, or lagging. A hunter who trusts a number over the world in front of them is making a mistake the world is entitled to punish.
