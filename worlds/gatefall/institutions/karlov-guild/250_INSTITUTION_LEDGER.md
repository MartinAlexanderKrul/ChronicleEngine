## Karlov Guild

Karlov Guild is Prague's dominant hunter guild by relationship rather than money — a family-run outfit operating out of a converted townhouse, Karlov House (ENT-000097), rather than a tower, with a guildmaster who remembers the Gatefall firsthand and a hallway of framed photographs proving it. It wins a disproportionate share of the city's hardest contracts, including the heritage-sensitive clears inside the Old Town Gate Cluster (ENT-000094), not because it outspends Brandhof, Zenith Group, or Horizon Guild — Europe — it doesn't, on any of them — but because a decade of municipal relationships still counts for something in a city this small.

### Interior Factions

**The Family** — the guildmaster's inner circle, largely blood relations and decades-long retainers, who built Karlov's standing on trust and continuity and see every foreign approach as a test of whether the guild still means what its name has always meant.

**The New Contracts Desk** — younger managers who argue the guild cannot out-relationship the internationals' checkbooks forever, and that some form of outside capital or partnership is the only way to keep Karlov's staff from being poached one hunter at a time.

The Family treats the New Contracts Desk's proposals as a slow surrender; the New Contracts Desk treats the Family's refusal to even negotiate as a guild that would rather lose gracefully than survive.

### What They Control

Karlov House (ENT-000097) and the archive of municipal relationships run out of its upper floor; a disproportionate share of the Old Town Gate Cluster's (ENT-000094) heritage-clearance contracts, won on trust and non-destructive doctrine rather than price; generations of standing with city officials that no amount of foreign capital can buy outright.

### What They Want

To keep winning Prague's hardest, most prestigious contracts without selling the relationships that make it possible; to hold onto its staff and its standing against three foreign guilds courting all of it at once; to survive as Prague's guild, not become a branch office of someone else's.

### Current Tensions

Karlov is under a three-way foreign siege it did not ask for and cannot outspend: Horizon Guild — Europe (ENT-000105) is hiring away its talent city by city, while Brandhof and Zenith Group both circle its clients and contracts with money Karlov has no answer for on paper. The routine poaching is the visible siege, but the guild's single greatest asset — and the real prize under all three approaches — is its standing contract-by-choice with the nation's one S-rank, Tereza Blažková (ENT-000114), who cannot be compelled and clears under the Karlov name because she chooses to; it is the exception that makes the whole siege worth mounting, since her walking would gut the guild in a single signature. Inside the guild, that pressure is exactly what has the Family and the New Contracts Desk at odds — whether Karlov survives this decade by refusing every one of the three, or by finally striking a deal with whichever of them offers the least humiliating terms.
