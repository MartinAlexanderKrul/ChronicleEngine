# Purpose and Status

Reikon is a **demonstration world** for Decision 051's world-diegetic System layer, in a contemporary gate-and-hunter register: a modern world where a litRPG "System" is real *within the fiction*, yet every action still resolves through Chronicle Engine's ordinary emergent simulation (Rules Sections 4–5). It shows that an aggressive litRPG experience can be delivered **entirely at the world layer**, through the Supernatural Phenomena Contract (Rules Section 11), with no engine change.

Reikon is an **original setting in a shared genre**. It uses only generic genre conventions — rifts that spawn monsters, awakened hunters ranked by tier, hunter guilds, a loot economy, a leveling interface — and no characters, organizations, cosmology, or story from any specific existing work.

This is a **draft**: names, tone, and history are a starting point to revise. The load-bearing content is the Ledger's Section 11 contract and the engine-compliance boundary.

Reikon is **not canonical world content** until instantiated with real identifiers from `system/ID_REGISTRY.md`. The official world remains Asterra; Verra remains the Prototype Alpha validation world.

# The World

Thirty-one years ago, the **Rupture** tore the first **Rifts** into the sky over a dozen cities at once — unstable portals that breathe monsters into the world and, if left too long, **collapse**, disgorging everything inside at once into the streets. In the same hour, a minority of ordinary people **Awakened**: they gained a private interface, the **Ledger**, and with it the capacity to fight what came through.

A generation later, the world has organized itself around both facts. States license and grade their Awakened; corporate **guilds** field hunting teams and trade in the **cores** drawn from slain monsters; researchers dissect what the Ledger *is*; and a mass movement insists the Rupture *meant* something and polices what may be learned about it. Ordinary life continues beneath all of it — but every city lives one uncleared Rift away from catastrophe.

# The Ledger (the System)

Only the **Awakened** bear a Ledger — perhaps one person in a few thousand. To its bearer alone it shows a **Grade** (overall rank), a spread of **Aspects** (Power, Reflex, Focus, Presence, and the like), **Marks** (learned skills and techniques), and a **Progression** track — the diegetic "experience" that fills as they grow. When a bearer has genuinely advanced, the Ledger announces it: a felt, luminous **Ascension** — the "level-up." Everyone else — the **Dormant** — sees nothing and gains nothing.

## The Ledger — Supernatural Phenomena Contract (Rules Section 11.2)

**Source / origin.** The Ledger and the Rifts arrived together at the Rupture, 31 years ago. The origin of both is genuinely unknown and bitterly disputed: a message or judgment from beyond (the Vigil), the physics of a wounded world not yet understood (the Institute), or an exploitable resource with no author at all (the guilds and states who simply *use* it). No account is proven; the Rupture left no explanation.

**Access.** Awakening is involuntary and rare — it cannot be chosen, bought, or reliably induced, and most people never Awaken. Once Awakened, **advancement** requires genuine ordeal, practice, study, and deeds, never mere wishing. **Classes** — recognized progression paths — may require training, guild sponsorship, or licensed instruction to formally "open," and the powerful ones are gatekept.

**Costs and limitations.** The Ledger **measures; it does not gift.** No Grade lets a bearer exceed what they can actually do — the impossible stays impossible (Rules Section 4.2). Advancement demands real cost: time, risk, injury, tutelage. Clearing trivial Rifts by rote yields nothing; the Ledger cannot be farmed without genuine growth. Marks must be learned and practiced (Rules Sections 5.3, 8); they are not bought with Progression.

**Risk.** Proportional and real. Rifts kill; a collapse can kill thousands; there is no plot armor (Rules Section 6). Rapid ascent draws danger — deadlier Rifts, rival hunters, a state's demands. Forbidden Classes and corrupted Marks inflict lasting harm (Character State, Rules Section 5.8).

**Discovery, teaching, transmission.** How the Ledger truly works is itself a **Knowledge Subject** (Rules Section 8): studied empirically by the Institute, codified into training by the guilds and the Authority, doctrinally fixed by the Vigil, and mythologized by the public. Understanding is uneven, contested, and frequently wrong.

**Social and institutional consequences.** Reikon is stratified by Grade. The Authority licenses and ranks the Awakened; guilds field them commercially and corner the core economy; the Vigil interprets the Rupture and pressures what may be taught; and a black market trades in illicit tutelage, unlicensed Marks, and **forged Ledger credentials**.

# Engine Compliance — the Ledger is a lens, not a resolver (Decision 051)

This is the boundary that keeps Reikon a Chronicle Engine world and not a different engine:

- **The Ledger is the world's diegetic representation of emergent capability, not a second resolution system.** When a character acts, resolution is still intent + circumstance + d100 (Rules Section 4) against demonstrated capability. A high Aspect *reflects* genuine established capability and functions as an ordinary **Modifier** (Rules Section 4.4) — never an override, gate, or auto-success.
- **Growth is still lived (Rules Section 5).** Clearing a Rift *is* real combat experience; the rising Progression bar and the Ascension are the Ledger's **display** of that emergent growth (Decision 051), never its cause. Growth precedes the level-up — never the reverse.
- **The readout is a claim, not objective truth (Rules Section 2.2).** A Ledger page — and any official Grade drawn from it — can be misread, spoofed, suppressed, or **forged** like any other information; it is Character and Public Knowledge, not Objective Reality. NPCs act on their *beliefs* about a hunter's Grade, which can be wrong — a raid that trusts a forged high Grade dies of the mistake.
- **No universal power pool.** Progression is a diegetic tally, not a spendable resource (consistent with Decisions 012, 013, 018; Rules Section 11.3).

In short: to its people, Reikon is a world of Grades, stats, and level-ups. To the engine, it is an ordinary emergent simulation wearing a diegetic interface. Both are true at once — which is precisely the world-first design the engine exists for.

# Grades (the ranking ladder)

The Authority grades licensed hunters on an original scale — **Grade V** (newly Awakened) up through **Grade I** (national-threat capable) — assessed from demonstrated deeds, not from the Ledger's private numbers alone. A rare few defy the scale and are classed **Anomalous**: Ledger pages with no clear ceiling, whose bearers can outgrow their origins entirely. An Anomalous awakening is the classic litRPG "a nobody who can rise without limit" — a generic archetype, to be given original execution in the campaign layer, with no fixed signature power assumed here.

# The Institutions (sketch)

Four contesting institutions, each an Institution under Rules Section 9, echoing the validator / restrictor / monopolist tension proven in Verra — but here the contested thing is the System and the Rifts themselves:

- **The Hunters' Authority** — the state body that licenses the Awakened, assigns Grades, and assigns Rift-clearance duty. The **credentialing and regulatory power**.
- **The Guilds** (e.g. the *Meridian Guild*, a leading example) — private hunting companies that field teams, hoard high-Grade talent, and corner the **core** market. The **commercial monopoly** interest.
- **The Aurology Institute** — the research body studying the Ledger and the Rifts empirically; its findings about their true nature keep colliding with Vigil doctrine. The **validator**.
- **The Vigil of the Rupture** — a mass movement holding that the Rupture was a judgment or message, which presses to suppress research it deems profane. The **restrictor**.

# The Present Situation (Year 31 AR)

Rift activity around the seat-of-play city of **Verholt** has been climbing for a year, and the last two collapses killed civilians the Authority swore to protect. The Institute has quietly recovered something from a deep Rift that contradicts the Vigil's account of the Rupture — and the Vigil knows. The dominant guild is buying up licenses and burying rivals. Into this rises a newly Awakened hunter (the protagonist, defined in the campaign layer), low-Graded and unproven, whose page may not be as ordinary as the Authority recorded.

# Notes for Reskinning

The setting flavor may be freely revised. Whatever the revision, three things must hold, or Reikon stops being a Chronicle Engine world:

1. The System **displays** capability; it never **grants** it (Decision 051 bright line).
2. Action resolution stays on Rules Section 4; growth stays on Rules Section 5.
3. The System's readouts — Grades, Aspects, credentials — are in-world **information** (Rules Section 2.2), not objective truth: they can be wrong, hidden, or forged.
