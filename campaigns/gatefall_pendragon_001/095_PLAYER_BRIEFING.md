# Who You Are

You are Alexander Pendragon, 25, three months into being one of the awakened. You graduated in mathematics and physics and spent your twenties teaching — part-time krav maga instruction, part-time in a classroom — until the Gates found you too. You cleared intake, you got your license, and a week ago it became active: E-rank, the bottom of the ladder.

You read situations correctly. Not usually — always. It's a cold, analytical habit of mind that doesn't miss much, and it's the one thing about you that isn't up for debate. What is up for debate is what you do with it: once you've read a room, you trust it completely and you move, often faster than anyone slower to see it would like.

# Why You Are Here

You're awakened and licensed. That's the job now. You've traded a classroom and a gym for a hunter's license and a city that runs on Gates, and you intend to actually do the work — not drift, not wait for someone to hand you a reason.

# What You Know

You know Chicago's hunting trade the way one week of licensing teaches it: BGM Region V runs licensing, grading, and contract auctions; the Windy City Hunters Coalition is where an unaffiliated freelancer like you registers; Horizon Guild and Ironline Guild are the two majors, and neither wants you. You know Gates open constantly across the metro, most of them E or D grade, and that a Gate left too long breaks and everything left inside comes out. You know death in a Gate is final — the trade doesn't pretend otherwise.

You've heard, the way everyone at the Coalition desk has heard, that you have a reputation already: word going around is that you're the weakest licensed E-rank in the city. Nobody's tested that against an actual clear. It's just what people say before they decline to take you.

# What You Have

$4,000 in the bank — a stable cushion, not hunting income. An active E-rank license about a week old. No hunter-grade gear of any kind: no graded weapon, no armor, nothing from a Gate, because you haven't been in one. What you do have is years of krav maga training and a body that knows how to fight, for whatever an ordinary human's fighting is worth against what's on the other side of a Gate.

You also have $30,000 in outstanding tuition debt, with a $400 minimum due every month. It doesn't touch your $4,000 — it's a pressure on your income, not a debt against your cash on hand.

# People and Institutions

You're registered with the Windy City Hunters Coalition's freelancer desk, but you haven't been taken onto a crew. No coordinator, crew leader, or fellow hunter has become someone you'd call a contact yet — just names on a roster and a reputation that keeps getting there before you do.

# What Matters Now

Every posted E or D contract requires a full five-hunter crew — there's no solo bid to fall back on, and insurance doesn't cover you if you tried anyway. Your immediate problem is getting a coordinator or a crew leader to actually take you on one, against a reputation you didn't earn and haven't had the chance to disprove. Behind that is the quieter pressure of $400 a month you need real income to keep meeting.

The first scene will not begin until you say you are ready.
