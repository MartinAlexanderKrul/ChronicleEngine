# The World

Thirty-one years ago, the **Rupture** tore open the first **Rifts** — pocket dimensions that appeared without warning over a dozen cities at once. In that same hour, a rare minority of ordinary people **Awakened**: they developed supernatural abilities and gained access to the **System**, an enigmatic interface showing their power and growth.

The world learned to adapt. Rifts are cordoned off by local government. Awakened hunters enter these pocket dungeons as expeditions, fight the monsters inside, and close the core to collapse the Rift. Cores — crystalline residues left by monsters and Rifts — became currency, medicine, and industry. Three powers emerged: civilian **Government** (administration and law), the **Hunters' Association** (licensing and ranking the Awakened), and **Guilds** (corporate hunter teams competing for contracts and cores).

Ideological movements exist — the **Vigil of the Rupture** and others — but they hold no institutional power. They shape belief and culture, not policy.

The city of **Verholt** is your seat of play: a thriving hub where hunter expeditions are constantly dispatched, cores trade hands, and the pressure is building. Rift activity has climbed for the past year. Two have already collapsed, killing hundreds. The System — how it works, who can access it, whether legends of its power are real — remains partially mythologized.

# Rifts and the System

## What are Rifts?

A **Rift** is a wound in the world — a gateway to a pocket dimension. When a Rift appears at a location (a building, a warehouse, underground, anywhere), the area is cordoned off immediately by government and local authorities. No one enters without authorization. Hunters register expeditions to enter and seal it.

Inside a Rift is a confined dungeon space: monsters, exploration, danger, and at its core a **core crystal** (an object that holds the pocket dimension open). To seal a Rift means reaching the core, destroying or sealing it from inside, and escaping. Once the core is sealed, the pocket dimension collapses *safely* — the Rift is gone, the location is secure. The core's crystalline remains become a **hunter core** — the valuable resource extracted from the expedition, traded for money, medicine, crafting, industry.

## The Rift Lifecycle and Timeframe

**Emergence:** Rifts appear **suddenly and without warning** — but not uniformly random. Over 31 years, a pattern has emerged:

- **Industrial zones** (factories, warehouses, mining areas) host ~60% of Rifts. Hypothesis: Rift mana resonates with concentrated industrial activity.
- **Boundaries and edges** (city outskirts, forest/city borders, cliff faces) host ~25%. Hypothesis: Dimensional weakness at transition zones.
- **Downtown/residential centers** host ~10% (rare). When they do, it's a public crisis.
- **Deep wilderness** (uninhabited, far from people) host ~5%. Often discovered late or not at all.

The Association maps known Rift hotspots and stations scouts accordingly. A Rift emerging in downtown Verholt (population dense, no warning) is catastrophic. A Rift in the industrial warehouse district is manageable — scouts respond quickly, evacuation is faster, Rift perimeter is established.

By the time hunters know about it, the 2-7 day clock is already running.

**Open and Cordoned:** A Rift is immediately sealed off from the public by government. Monsters exist only inside the pocket dimension; they do not leak into the physical world. The core is intact and stable. The Association estimates the Rift's stability window (2-7 days) and posts the expedition assignment.

**Deepening Window: 2–7 days maximum.** Once opened, a Rift has between 2 and 7 days before the core reaches critical strength and the deepening process becomes irreversible. Most Rifts allow roughly 7 days; some progress faster (2-3 days). The Association must organize, staff, and execute an expedition within this window. There are no exceptions — no Rift remains stable longer than 7 days.

**Deepening:** If no expedition seals the core within the window, the core grows stronger, the interior expands, monsters worsen. The mana pressure increases. The deepening becomes unstoppable.

**Collapse (Catastrophic Failure):** The Rift ruptures from the inside. The pocket dimension breaks open violently. Every monster inside, plus destabilized core energy, spills into the physical world. Disaster. The **Danesmoor collapse** killed hundreds when a Rift's deepening window was missed or misjudged. The **Kessler Rift** is currently within its window; if not sealed in the next few days, it will follow.

**Prevention and Response:** The Hunters' Association tracks Rift emergence, estimates danger, and assigns expeditions to seal cores before deepening reaches critical. They answer to the public when their assessment is wrong. Danesmoor was a political and humanitarian disaster — the Authority's credibility is strained because they failed to prevent it.

## Expedition Failure States

Not every Rift is cleared. Teams face failure conditions:

**Forced Evacuation** — A team inside a Rift judges the core unreachable or the monster threat too high. They retreat and exit before time runs out. The Rift remains open, core intact, countdown continuing. A failed expedition can be reattempted, but the Rift is now higher priority and more dangerous (deepening has progressed).

**Core Unreachable** — A team reaches the core location but cannot destroy or seal it before running out of time/mana. They retreat empty-handed. The team receives no bonus pay, only base compensation. Reputation damage if it's a recurring pattern.

**Partial Clear** — A team clears some sections of a Rift, harvests some cores, but doesn't reach the core crystal. They collect loot but fail the objective. The Rift deepens anyway. Partial clears are tactically risky — good short-term profit, long-term rift danger.

**Cascading Failures** — Multiple teams fail to clear the same Rift. Public anxiety rises. The Association escalates, assigning higher-tier hunters or calling in Guild resources. A Rift with multiple failures is politically dangerous — Danesmoor was exactly this scenario.

**Consequences:**
- **Financial:** No objective bonus; lost time and resources.
- **Reputational:** Teams with high failure rates get lower-priority assignments or pay cuts.
- **Institutional:** Failed expeditions fuel public doubt in the Association's competence. Danesmoor is the cautionary tale.

**In play:** Failure is real and carries weight. A team that fails a mission faces consequence.

## Rift-Touched Zones

After a Rift is sealed, the area retains residual mana for weeks. This creates environmental anomalies:

**Flora & Fauna Mutations** — Plants grow unusually fast or twist into strange shapes. Local animals develop odd behaviors or minor mutations (color changes, unusual aggression, enhanced senses). These fade as the mana dissipates.

**Mana Echoes** — Hunters or residents sometimes sense faint "ghost" impressions of Rift monsters in the zone. Not dangerous, but unsettling. Some sensitive individuals report dreams or visions.

**Temporary Mana Availability** — A Rift-touched zone concentrates ambient mana. Hunters resting or meditating in the zone recover mana faster for the first 2-3 weeks. Specialized hunters use this to accelerate recovery between expeditions.

**Behavioral Patterns** — Animals avoid or cluster in Rift-touched zones based on mana resonance. Hunters learn to read these patterns for rift location prediction.

**Lore & Exploration** — Hunters investigate Rift-touched zones to study residual effects, collect rare materials, or search for missed loot. These zones become points of interest and story hooks.

**Timeline:** Effects fade over 2-6 weeks depending on Rift grade and environmental factors. A-Rift zones take longest; E-Rift zones clear quickly.

## Rift Grades, Frequency, and Monster Ecology

Rifts emerge with a grade that matches the monsters inside. Rift grade = monster grade inside; no mixing. Lower-grade monsters do not coexist with higher-grade ones (predation, territorial hierarchy).

Hunters are matched to Rift grades by the Association — C-Rank hunters enter C-Rifts, not E-Rifts or A-Rifts. A hunter badly mismatched to a Rift's grade dies.

**Baseline Frequency in Verholt:**

| Grade | Frequency | Monster Grade | Typical Team | Notes |
|-------|-----------|---|--------------|-------|
| **E-Rift** | Multiple per day | E-grade (weak) | Solo or supervised pair | Routine, low risk |
| **D-Rift** | Multiple per day | D-grade | 2-3 hunters, D Rank | Standard work |
| **C-Rift** | Several per week | C-grade (serious) | Team of 3-4, C Rank minimum | Common but not daily |
| **B-Rift** | Several per week | B-grade (dangerous) | Specialized team, B Rank | Careful planning required |
| **A-Rift** | ~1 per 29 days | A-grade (elite threat) | Veteran team, A Rank lead | Rare; high stakes |
| **S-Rift** | ~1 per year maximum | S-grade (unknown) | Elite team or solo S-Rank | Extremely rare; no rules apply |

**Baseline:** At least one Rift per day emerges in Verholt, mostly E-D grade. The city runs on constant expedition work — the Association and Guilds field multiple teams daily, and cores flow steadily into the economy.

**Exceptions and Danger:**
- **Grade shifts:** An established Rift's grade can increase during deepening. An E-Rift being delayed can shift to D or higher, trapping a team inside with suddenly stronger monsters. This is a real catastrophe risk.
- **S-Rank Rifts are lawless:** S-Rifts follow no established rules. Monster types, Rift behavior, internal structure, core location — all unknown. Anything can happen. S-Rift expeditions are legendary events, not routine work.

**The crisis:** Danesmoor (collapse) and Kessler (A-grade deepening) are **exceptions** where the system failed. Most days, the baseline holds. These are the breaks.

## Hunter Casualties and Career Dynamics

Being a hunter is **extremely dangerous.** Annual casualty and retirement rate is **20-30%** — deaths from Rift failures, injuries that end careers, and hunters who simply leave the profession after seeing what it costs.

**Consequences:**
- The profession is constantly bleeding people; the city needs constant flows of newly Awakened to maintain workforce
- Hunter shortage at higher tiers is real — A-Rank and S-Rank hunters are national assets, not replaceable
- E-D Rank hunters are common (easier to replace) but even they die regularly
- Career longevity is short; most hunters don't last decades
- This drives urgency in recruitment and the willingness to take risks

## Newly Awakened: Social Position and Value

A person who has just Awakened occupies an interesting social position:

**Desperately Wanted** — Awakened are rare. A new Awakened represents potential workforce. The Association wants to license and track them. Guilds want to recruit them. Mentors want to train them. A newly Awakened person suddenly has leverage and attention.

**Assumed Weak** — A new Awakened has no combat history. Rank tests are their only proof of capability. Until proven, even a D-Rank newly Awakened is treated as unreliable and inexperienced. Street credibility is zero.

**Wildcard** — New Awakened are unknown quantities. No one knows how their powers will manifest under real pressure. Some shine. Some break. This uncertainty makes them both interesting (recruitment bids) and risky (assignment caution).

**Entry-Level Salary Safety** — A newly Awakened licensed by the Association gets baseline pay and safe assignment options. The Association's salary floor means they can survive. Independent Awakened struggle.

**Target for Predators** — Fresh Awakened are also targets for forgers, fixers, and illegal hunting operations. A lonely new Awakened in a tavern might be approached with offers for fast money or fast-track guild sponsorship. These are traps.

**Social Variation by Rank** — A newly Awakened E-Rank is treated as normal entry-level. A newly Awakened C-Rank or higher is rare and gets celebrity treatment — guilds court them, media notices them, governments may take interest.

**In play:** You are a newly Awakened hunter. You have leverage (you're rare), but also vulnerability (you're unproven, and predators are watching). Your first expeditions will define your reputation.

## Rank Forgery and the Self-Evident Check

Forged Ranks exist but are **rare and suppressed**. The self-enforcing mechanism: if you forge a high Rank and enter a Rift above your actual Rank, the monsters will kill you. Your skill level doesn't match your certified Rank, and everyone around you will know immediately that you were a fraud. The price is death.

Some hunters take the risk anyway (desperation, ambition), but the Association hunts forgers. The consequences if caught: execution or permanent Rank revocation (banning from all Rift work).

Forged Ranks are rare because the death penalty is immediate and obvious.

## Hunter Payment: Salary + Bonus Model

Hunters are paid through a **rank-based salary system with performance bonuses:**

- **Base salary:** Determined by official Rank. E-Rank hunters earn less than S-Rank hunters; the gap is significant.
- **Expedition bonus:** Successful Rift clearance yields additional pay. Bonuses scale with Rift grade (clearing a C-Rift pays more bonus than an E-Rift).
- **Employer variation:** The **Hunters' Association** uses this salary + bonus model. **Guilds** may negotiate differently (higher base, lower bonus, or per-contract rates). **Independent hunters** work per-expedition rates, no salary.
- **Income reality:** A steady E-Rank hunter in the Association can survive; an independent E-Rank struggles. Higher Ranks (B-A-S) are well-compensated and can demand better terms.

The system incentivizes hunters to clear higher-grade Rifts (for bonuses) but keeps entry-level hunters in the Association system (for the salary safety net).

## Rift Team Composition Requirements

Rifts must be cleared by **teams with proper Rank composition:**

**Core rule:** The **majority of the team must be at the same Rank as the Rift** (or higher).
- A C-Rift needs mostly C-Rank hunters (C-Rank majority). You can include some D-Rank or lower **as support** — carrying gear, logistics, field first aid — but they don't lead and don't fight the monsters.
- B-Rift needs mostly B-Rank hunters; can include some A-Rank as leaders and C-Rank/lower in support.

**Flexibility:**
- A lower-Rank hunter can participate in a Rift **one tier above** their Rank (D-Rank in a C-Rift, C-Rank in a B-Rift) if the majority rule is satisfied. Risk is theirs; monsters don't care about your intent.
- A lower-Rank hunter can participate in **higher-tier Rifts** (more than one tier above) **only in support roles** — carrying equipment, setting camp, evacuating injured, managing resources. They do not engage monsters directly.

**Rationale:** This prevents catastrophic team wipe-outs and ensures appropriate firepower for each Rift grade.

## The System

**Almost nobody has the System.** An Awakened hunter has mana, Abilities, and a certified Rank — and no interface of any kind. They know their own power the way a swordsman knows their arm: by using it. Nothing counts their growth, nothing announces it, and no number tells them what they are. That is the ordinary condition of every hunter in Reikon.

The **System** is a private, luminous interface perceived by a **System Bearer** alone. To them it shows:

- **Rank** — their tier (E through S, with rare **Anomalous** breaking the scale)
- **Abilities** — the supernatural power(s) they Awakened with, plus any learned techniques (called **Marks**)
- **Mana reserves** — their capacity before exhaustion
- **Growth** — experience that climbs as they face real danger and challenge

It does not merely watch. When its Bearer earns enough XP through resolved combat and ordeal, the System causes an **Ascension** — a felt, luminous milestone that *grants* progression under `RKO-OVR-001`: stat points, unlocks, and a deeper mana pool. You cannot level up without genuine challenge, but the threshold crossing and its grants are causally real.

The System also intervenes. It may grant a one-use technique, issue a dangerous Objective, open a trial Rift, or—rarely—displace its Bearer into an unknown trial without asking. These interventions never grant victory or free XP; they create ordeals whose outcomes still resolve normally. Its purpose is to produce a stronger Bearer, not to protect the current person carrying it.

**Important:** the System shows its Bearer their actual capability. Their **official Rank** — what the Association certifies, what the Guilds know — is based on testing and assessment instead. These can diverge, and for a Bearer they inevitably will. A forged Rank, an underestimated hunter, an inflated reputation — all are possible and dangerous.

## Awakening

Awakening is involuntary and rare — roughly 1 per few thousand people. It cannot be chosen, induced, or bought. When it happens, a person wakes with one or more supernatural abilities (strength, speed, magic, healing, Rift-sensing, etc.) and a mana pool that will not change for the rest of their life.

**Most Awakened** have a single ability or a focused set. They Rank from E (newly Awakened, can clear basic dungeons) through S (elite, decades of proven power). They have no System, no level, and no XP. They grow the slow way — practice, ordeal, and mastery — and get dangerous without ever being told they have.

**The System-Bearer Legend:** once per generation, maybe less, an Awakened is said to wake with something else entirely — an interface that quantifies them and answers back. A Bearer's growth is not merely tracked but *granted*: they can theoretically reach heights no ordinary hunter can match, and their mana pool, alone among the living, **deepens**. This has happened so rarely in 31 years that it is half-mythology. Most hunters consider it a story hunters tell about hunters.

Only one living Bearer exists in a world-line at a time. If that Bearer dies, the System does not resurrect them or inherit their strength. It waits through an unknown interval and eventually chooses another person. The successor begins as themselves, with none of the dead Bearer's levels, memories, allocations, or possessions. The System is patient enough to lose a host; mortals are not.

**No one knows whether a System-Bearer currently exists. If one does, the Association, the Guilds, the Institute, and the Vigil all want to know — and none of them want the same thing from the answer.**

*(One does. See `campaigns/reikon_awakening_001/`. The world does not know it, and nothing in world canon records it — the Bearer's own ledger is the only place it is written.)*

# Engine Compliance — World Rule Profile and shared resolution

This boundary keeps Reikon a Chronicle Engine world:

- **The System causes capability growth but is not an action resolver.** XP thresholds, Ascensions, point grants, and unlocks are causal under `RKO-OVR-001`. When a character acts, resolution is still intent + circumstance + d100 (Rules Section 4); established Stats and Abilities determine modifier steps and available methods, not the rolled outcome.
- **System vitality is canonical.** Health and percentage damage reduction operate under `RKO-OVR-002`; wounds, pain, shock, and conditions may coexist with the numeric pool.
- **Ranks are claims, not objective truth (Rules Section 2.2).** An official Rank or hunter credential can be misread, forged, or wrong. NPCs act on their *beliefs* about a hunter's Rank, which can be mistaken — trusting a forged high Rank can be lethal.
- **No universal power pool.** Abilities draw on mana reserves (a diegetic tally), not a spendable currency (consistent with Decisions 012, 013, 018; Rules Section 11.3).

In short: Reikon is a world of causal Ranks, stats, Health, and Ascensions, expressed through an explicit World Rule Profile while retaining Chronicle Engine's shared resolution and persistence contracts.

# Critical Discovery: The System Has an Architect

The **Aurology Institute** recently recovered evidence from a deep Rift proving that the System was deliberately designed — it was not a natural phenomenon or accident of the Rupture. Someone or something created the System, and it arrived with the Rifts 31 years ago.

This directly contradicts the **Vigil of the Rupture's** theology, which frames the Rupture and System as divine judgment or cosmic message. The Vigil is pressuring suppression of the Institute's findings. The implications are profound: if the System was made, by whom? For what purpose? Can it be unmade, controlled, or understood?

This is the current institutional crisis driving all four power centers.

# Hunter Ranks (E–S)

The **Hunters' Association** certifies and ranks all licensed Awakened on a ladder:

| Rank | Meaning | Typical Rift Assignment |
|------|---------|------------------------|
| **E** | Newly Awakened; basic training complete | E-Rift solo or supervised |
| **D** | Competent solo hunter; proven through field assessment | D-Rift solo; E-Rift team lead |
| **C** | Experienced; handles moderate danger | C-Rift team member; B-Rift with backup |
| **B** | Veteran; rare and sought after | B-Rift lead; A-Rift with experienced team |
| **A** | Elite; public reputation and heavy demand | A-Rift lead; S-Rift with team |
| **S** | Legendary; top-tier national threat-capable | S-Rift lead; Special assignments |
| **Anomalous** | Breaks the scale; growth potential unclear | Unassigned; tracked by all factions |

## S-Rank Hunters: Heroes and Uncontained

S-Rank hunters are **extremely rare** — only a handful exist in the entire nation. They are so powerful that no institution can force their obedience; only other S-Rank hunters can truly contain or oppose them.

Consequently, S-Rank hunters are treated as **national heroes and public figures.** They command respect, fear, and cultural reverence. Their names are known; their deeds are legendary. They are celebrities — media attention follows them, civilians recognize them, governments treat them with diplomatic caution.

An S-Rank hunter is effectively **independent** — they work where they choose. The Association can request their services; they accept or refuse based on their own judgment. Guilds court them as anchors of reputation. Governments listen to their opinions. An S-Rank hunter who disagrees with institutional policy cannot be compelled; disagreement itself becomes a political crisis.

This power imbalance is foundational: S-Rank hunters are assets the world needs but cannot control. It shapes how they are treated, how they live, and how they interact with the structures around them. An S-Rank hunter's decision affects nations.

**Awakening and Rank are immutable.** People can Awaken at any point in their life, but most Awaken **after age 14**. When an Awakened person awakens, they emerge with a fixed **mana pool** that determines their Rank permanently. You cannot be promoted to a higher Rank; your Rank at Awakening is your Rank for life.

- An E-Rank Awakened will always be E-Rank.
- A C-Rank Awakened will always be C-Rank.
- Advancement does not exist in this world — Rank is destiny.

**Awakening as a life event:** A person might live 20, 30, or 40 years without Awakening, then suddenly Awaken with a supernatural ability and a Rank. This can happen at any moment. Some Awaken as children (rare), most after puberty, some in middle age or later. There is no pattern or warning.

**Flexibility:** Higher-Rank hunters commonly take lower-Rank work. An A-Rank can enter an E-Rift if needed (it's trivially easy for them). This keeps the system flexible and prevents shortage problems at entry levels.

**Official certification:** The Association tests newly Awakened to determine their Rank from demonstrated mana pool, then certifies them with an official license. A hunter's **System Rank** (what they privately see) and **Official Rank** (what the Association certified) should match, but a misread or corrupted record can diverge. This gap is dangerous — a forged high Rank means you die in the Rift.

Rare **Anomalous** hunters show unlimited potential that breaks the scale — the rarest classification, deeply watched and contested.

# The Four Power Centers

**Government** — Civilian administration. Cities, law, infrastructure, cordoning Rifts, public order. The Association answers to government; Guilds are licensed by it. Government is *functional* but often plays catch-up to Rift crises.

**Hunters' Association** — The state licensing body. Tests, ranks, and certifies all Awakened hunters (E through S). Posts Rift assignments, mediates disputes, enforces hunting law, and maintains the public face of organized Rift clearance. The Association's credibility is currently strained after the Danesmoor collapse and rising Rift activity.

*Association Rift Detection and Scouts:* The Association maintains a **scout corps** — experienced hunters stationed in hotspot zones (industrial districts, edges, known weak spots). When a Rift emerges (tremor, energy spike, citizen report, Rift-Sensor alert), scouts respond within minutes. They:
  - Confirm Rift emergence and location
  - Estimate the Rift's grade (by pressure, monster sensing, environmental clues)
  - Establish initial perimeter and evacuation zones
  - Report to Association HQ with all findings
  - Brief the expedition team before deployment

Response time is critical. A Rift detected within 30 minutes gives the team most of the 2-7 day window. A Rift discovered late (6 days in, deepening advanced) is a crisis.

*Association Politics:* Different Association captains and regional directors play favorites. Some steer high-paying, safe assignments to preferred teams (friends, guild-affiliated, politically connected hunters). Others push disfavored teams into high-risk, low-reward work. A hunter's relationship with local Association leadership directly affects which Rifts they're offered, how much they're paid, and how much support they receive. This creates incentive for hunters to cultivate relationships, trade favors, and navigate institutional politics. A new, unconnected Awakened hunter gets base assignments with no preference — potentially riskier than an established hunter's network would guarantee.

**Guilds** — Private hunting organizations. Field teams of hunters, negotiate contracts, trade cores, compete for high-Rank talent and lucrative assignments. The **Meridian Guild** is the largest and most powerful; smaller independent guilds also operate. Guilds are profitable and efficient but answer only to their shareholders, not public safety.

**Aurology Institute** — The research authority on Rifts and Awakening. Conducts empirical studies on Rift mechanics, origin, and danger assessment; on the Awakening phenomenon itself; and on how hunters develop Abilities and grow through real challenge. They observe and document the System's effects (Ascensions, growth, Ability emergence) but do not understand the System itself — it remains enigmatic even to them. Recently recovered something from a deep Rift that contradicts the Vigil's account of the Rupture's origin — likely related to how Rifts began or why Awakening occurs. The Vigil has learned of it and is pressuring suppression. The Institute has institutional credibility and government funding, but its researchers are politically vulnerable when their findings challenge powerful beliefs.

## Ideological Movements (no institutional power)

**The Vigil of the Rupture** — A mass movement holding that the Rupture was a judgment, message, or cosmic event with spiritual meaning. They pressure what research is "acceptable," protest certain Rift assignments, and shape public narrative. They hold no institutional authority but are culturally influential and can make life difficult for those who contradict their doctrine.

Other belief systems and conspiracy theories exist but lack organized followings.

# The Underground: Unlicensed Hunting and Gray Markets

Not all Rift expeditions are official. A shadow economy of unlicensed hunters, gray-market Rift operations, and core smuggling exists parallel to the Association and Guilds.

## Unlicensed Hunters

Hunters who operate without Association licenses work for a variety of reasons:

- **Desperation** — Failed to rank high enough; earn more money faster without Association salary caps.
- **Independence** — Refuse institutional control; prefer freedom over safety.
- **Criminality** — Hunted by the Association for forgery, violence, or theft.
- **Refugee or foreign status** — Cannot get licensed without citizenship; work illegally.

Unlicensed hunters are paid per-expedition by independent contractors or gray-market fixers. Typical rates: 20-30% less than Association base pay, but no salary floor and no job security.

## Gray-Market Rift Operations

Underground networks post Rift assignments outside official channels:

- **How it works** — A fixer learns of a Rift (through scouts, citizens, or stolen Association intel). They assemble an unlicensed team and clear it before the Association can. Quick profit, high risk.
- **Legality** — Illegal. Operating a Rift without Association permission is a felony.
- **Risk** — Unlicensed teams get no official support, briefing, or equipment backup. Casualties are higher. Failed Rifts mean lost cores and deaths with no compensation.
- **Profit** — A successful gray-market clear yields 100% core profit (Association takes no cut). A single C-Rift (15-30 cores) can be split among 3-4 hunters for life-changing money.

## Core Smuggling

Cores stolen or harvested illegally are fenced through black-market buyers:

- **Buyers** — Criminal syndicates, foreign governments, private collectors, academic thieves.
- **Price** — 70-80% of official value (risk markup).
- **Use** — Cores are rare and valuable everywhere; every city has underground demand.

A hunter who steals a Rift's harvest or diverts official cores can make significant money quickly — but the Association hunts core thieves mercilessly.

## Association Crackdown

The Association pursues unlicensed operations aggressively:

- Raids on unlicensed team safehouses
- Arrest warrants for known fixers
- Execution for habitual core theft
- Mandatory licenses for anyone Awakened (no opt-out)

However, the Association is overstretched. Rising Rift activity means they can't police everything. Underground operations still thrive in the margins, especially in less-regulated districts or during crises (when Association resources are focused on major Rifts).

## In Play

A new Awakened hunter might be approached by fixers offering quick money and independence. The tradeoff is immediate risk (no safety net, better odds of death) and legal risk (if caught, execution or lifetime ban). Some hunters view it as entrepreneurial; others see it as a death wish.

# The Present Situation (Year 31 AR)

Rift activity in and around **Verholt** (population ~900,000) has been climbing for the past year. Two Rifts have already collapsed in the region — Danesmoor among them — killing hundreds and shaking public confidence in the Association's competence. The **Kessler Rift** in the industrial district is currently deepening and approaching critical; the pressure to clear it before collapse is mounting, and the Authority is scrambling to assemble a capable team.

The **Aurology Institute** has quietly recovered something from a deep Rift — a core or artifact with properties that contradict the Vigil of the Rupture's theological account of the Rupture's origin. The Vigil has learned of it and is applying pressure to suppress the findings. # Optional Advanced Mechanics

The following features enhance Reikon gameplay but are not required. Add them if they resonate; leave them out if they complicate play.

**Monster Classification & Learning** — Hunters learn to identify monster archetypes (Weaklings, Bruisers, Swarmers, Hunters, etc.) within each grade. Recognizing an enemy type allows adapted tactics and builds progression through knowledge, not just raw power.

**Core Crafting & Enhancement** — Rare cores don't just trade for money; they go to specialists who craft unique weapons, armor, or ability-enhancing relics. A Strength-core might become gauntlets boosting physical power; a Rift-Sensing core becomes a warning pendant. Loot discovery drives character customization.

**Expedition Failure States** — Not every Rift gets cleared. Teams sometimes evacuate, leaving the core intact. Failures damage reputation, cost money, and have narrative weight. Creates moral stakes and consequence.

**Team Bonds & Synergy** — Teams that work together over time unlock combo attacks, shared mana tactics, or coordinated techniques. Incentivizes long-term party formation and emotional investment in NPCs.

**Association Politics** — Association captains favor certain hunters, steering better assignments and pay to preferred teams. Others face high-risk pushes or low-reward assignments. Creates party dynamics and relationship complexity.

**Rift-Touched Zones** — Cleared Rifts leave residual mana in the area for weeks. Causes strange phenomena: plant mutations, animal oddities, minor Rift echoes. Creates exploration opportunities and world flavor.

**Rift Sensing Ability** — Some Awakened develop heightened Rift awareness: feeling instability, sensing monsters through the seal, predicting timing. A specialist skill that makes certain hunters irreplaceable for early-warning or emergency response.

The System is Reikon's diegetic interface and a causal progression/vitality system under its World Rule Profile, not a second action-resolution system. Uncertain actions remain governed by Rules Section 4. Official assessments can be wrong, forged, or misleading; canonical System state is real for its bearer.
