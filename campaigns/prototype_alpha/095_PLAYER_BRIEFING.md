# Who You Are

You are Ilse Varn, a recently licensed physician and scholar. Your training has taught you medicine, close observation, and how to weigh evidence, but your license does not give you influence in Halden. You arrive as an outsider of modest means, travel-worn but healthy, with no local reputation and no special protection from the consequences of your choices.

Ilse is curious, principled, and cautious in unfamiliar company. Those are starting tendencies, not a script. How she responds to pressure, whom she trusts, and what kind of physician she becomes are yours to decide.

# Why You Are Here

You have come to Halden hoping to study at its Athenaeum. A mentor from your former college has given you a letter introducing you to Fellow Corvane, a scholar whose recent claim about the seasonal marsh-fever has divided the town.

Your immediate task is simple in principle: reach the Athenaeum, present the letter, and secure permission to work. In practice, you have arrived at a politically dangerous moment.

# What You Know

Marsh-fever returns with the warm season. You have heard Corvane's claim that tainted water spreads it, and you know the Athenaeum considers his evidence convincing. You also know the Order of the Ember has condemned the teaching as heresy and has civic authority in Halden.

You have not examined Corvane's evidence. You do not yet know whether his claim is correct, and you have formed no settled opinion of your own. Around town, confidence, fear, doctrine, professional rivalry, and rumor are difficult to separate.

You also know that Halden is famous for unusually durable glass controlled by its Glaziers' Guild. You know the product by reputation, not how it is made.

# What You Have

You carry your physician's instrument case, the letter of introduction, a modest purse, travel clothes, and ordinary personal effects. Your medical license is genuine, but it does not guarantee employment, lodging, institutional access, or deference from local authorities.

# People and Institutions

Fellow Corvane knows of you only through the letter; you have not met. The Halden Athenaeum is your intended destination and possible professional foothold. The Order of the Ember is a powerful religious and civic authority whose position on the fever dispute cannot safely be ignored. The Glaziers' Guild is publicly known for its craft and monopoly, but you have no relationship with it.

# What Matters Now

The first fever cases of the season are appearing in a poor river ward just as the Order increases pressure on the Athenaeum. Before long, you may have to decide what medical advice you are willing to give when the evidence is limited and the authorities disagree.

Nothing requires you to accept Corvane's view, defy the Order, or pursue a heroic course. Your first concern may be professional footing, patient care, personal safety, evidence, or something else entirely.
