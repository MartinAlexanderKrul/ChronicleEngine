# Halden Recent History — Verra

**Ledger:** Canonical Record (`011` Section 8) — Historical Layer (300–399). A world-scope **historical record** of recent events in Halden.

This is an **in-world historical document** in the sense of `001_ENGINE_DECISIONS.md` Decision 038 and `010_ENGINE_RULES.md` Section 12: it records what Halden knows and remembers, not privileged truth. Its account of public events is reliable; it does not resolve disputed questions (the true cause of the marsh-fever remains unestablished), and it holds only what was publicly observable — not the private reasoning or intent of the people involved.

It is the durable world-layer memory promoted from the **closed Prototype Alpha campaign** (`campaigns/prototype_alpha/`) when that campaign ended with its protagonist's death. The campaign's own ledgers remain as historical evidence of her life; this record is what persists in the world after the campaign closed, so later campaigns in Verra inherit the consequences. Provenance for the affair traces to campaign event `EVT-000013`.

## The Varn Affair (Late Spring, Year 312 RR)

In the late spring of Year 312 RR, an outsider physician named **Ilse Varn** (`ENT-000018`) arrived in Halden, newly licensed and without local standing. She came in connection with the Athenaeum's (`ENT-000003`) disputed marsh-fever finding (`ENT-000013`) and was known to have sought out Fellow Corvane (`ENT-000007`).

Within days she was drawn into the standing conflict between the Athenaeum and the Order of the Ember (`ENT-000004`). The Order, under Prelate Solenne (`ENT-000008`), recruited her into its service as a consulting physician. That arrangement collapsed almost immediately: on her first day at the Order's hospice she assaulted a guard, and shortly after attacked Prelate Solenne herself, attempting to strangle her. She was detained.

Offered continued service under confinement, she refused. She was handed to Halden's city watch, tried before the magistrate for assault on an Order member and attempted murder of the Prelate, and **publicly executed by hanging** in the Halden public square in the early summer of Year 312 RR, witnessed by hundreds. The execution is widely known in Halden and stands publicly as what befalls those who raise a hand against the Order.

What drove her is not publicly settled. In the streets it is variously told: that she was unstable; that she was an Athenaeum partisan who lost her nerve; that the Order broke her. Her private reasons, her dealings with Corvane, and whatever she concluded about the fever are not part of the public record — only the outcome is.

**Provenance:** promoted from the closed Prototype Alpha campaign; the originating event is `EVT-000013`. Ilse Varn (`ENT-000018`) is a persistent entity whose canonical record was that campaign; she is retained here as a deceased figure in Halden's history.

## The Marsh-Fever Outbreak (Current — Early Summer, Year 312 RR)

Through the late spring of Year 312 RR an outbreak of the marsh-fever spread in Halden's river ward, rising from a handful of cases to **thirteen or more** confirmed by early summer, concentrated in the lower quarters. That people are sick, and where, is observable fact.

**The cause remains disputed and unestablished.** The Athenaeum's validation of Corvane's water-borne finding (`EVT-000005`) and the Order's condemnation of it as heresy (`EVT-000006`) both stand; neither the affair nor this record resolves whether the finding is true. The outbreak sharpened the dispute but did not settle it. Objective truth of the transmission is not recorded anywhere as fact.

## Institutional Aftermath

- **The Order of the Ember (`ENT-000004`, Prelate Solenne `ENT-000008`)** emerged publicly vindicated: it absorbed an assault on its Prelate and answered it through lawful process, reinforcing its civic authority. Solenne bore visible injury from the attack.
- **The Athenaeum (`ENT-000003`, Rector Maelis `ENT-000006`)** remains under pressure over the fever finding, and its brief association with the executed physician is a liability it would prefer forgotten.
- **Fellow Corvane (`ENT-000007`)** continues to hold and defend the finding; its teaching remains banned within Halden. His research is neither validated beyond the Athenaeum's standard nor abandoned.

These are the durable, publicly-known conditions a newcomer to Halden would encounter in the early summer of Year 312 RR.
