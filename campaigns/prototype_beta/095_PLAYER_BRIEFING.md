# Who You Are

You are Kael Travis, a wandering soldier and sword-for-hire in your late thirties. Years on the road have left you weathered, guarded, and dangerous. You are a master swordsman: trained, experienced, and reliable when steel is out and fear is in the room.

Kael's anger is real. It can make him decisive under pressure, but it has also cost him stability, trust, and probably more than one employer. That temperament is a starting condition, not a script. What he does with it is yours.

# Why You Are Here

You have come to Halden looking for paid work and, if fortune is less cruel than usual, something more settled than another road contract. Halden is tense enough to need blades and proud enough to resent them.

The Order of the Ember has real civic-religious authority here. The Athenaeum is under pressure. The Glaziers' Guild has money and secrets. Fever is spreading in the lower quarters. Any of them might need protection, intimidation, escort, quiet enforcement, or a disposable outsider.

# What You Know

You know the public story: an outsider physician named Ilse Varn assaulted Prelate Solenne and was publicly hanged. Hundreds saw it. The lesson told in the streets is simple: raise a hand against the Order, and Halden will watch you die.

You know fever is active in the lower river ward. You have heard there is a dispute about its cause. The Athenaeum backs a forbidden claim about tainted water; the Order condemns teaching it. You do not know whether the claim is true.

You know Halden is famous for unusually durable glass controlled by its Glaziers' Guild. You know the product by reputation, not how it is made.

You have no special knowledge of the old rivermason mortar or Berrec beyond anything you might learn in play.

# What You Have

You carry a serviceable sword, a belt knife, worn traveling gear, a bedroll, simple blade-maintenance tools, and enough coin for a few modest days if you do not waste it.

You do not begin with a patron, civic office, license, armor of note, or legal protection beyond whatever restraint Halden usually shows to armed outsiders.

# People and Institutions

You have not personally met Rector Maelis, Fellow Corvane, Prelate Solenne, Guildmaster Torv, Master Glazier Della, or Berrec.

The Order matters because it has authority and because a recent public execution strengthened that authority. The Athenaeum matters because its dispute with the Order may create work or danger. The Guild matters because money, guarded craft, and monopoly often need sharp edges nearby.

# What Matters Now

Your immediate problem is practical: find work before your coin thins. Your deeper problem is whether you can turn skill with a sword into a position that lasts, in a town where anger has already killed one outsider.

The first scene will not begin until you say you are ready.
